Understanding the Legislative Stages

How a bill travels from introduction to becoming law in Kenya's Senate.

1

First Reading

Bill is formally introduced in the Senate. No debate at this stage — it is published in the Kenya Gazette for public notice.

2

Second Reading

The Senate debates the general principles of the bill. A vote decides whether it proceeds. Bills can be negatived at this stage.

3

Committee of the Whole Senate (CoWT)

The full Senate reviews the bill clause by clause. Amendments are debated and adopted.

4

Third Reading / Passed Senate

Final vote in the Senate. If passed, the bill moves to the National Assembly for their concurrence.

5

At National Assembly

The bill has been passed by the Senate and sent to the National Assembly. They may pass it with or without amendments, or reject it.

What happens next depends on whether both houses agree
6a

Mediation Committee If houses disagree

A joint Senate–National Assembly Mediation Committee is formed to agree a common version acceptable to both chambers.

6b

Presidential Memorandum If President objects

After a bill is passed by both Houses, the President may refer it back for reconsideration with a memorandum outlining reservations.

7

Assented into Law ✓

The President signs the bill. It becomes an Act of Parliament and part of Kenyan law upon the date of assent.
