20 · Mediation Deep Dive

{{ mediation_bills|length }} bills are currently in a Mediation Committee — a joint Senate–National Assembly process formed when both houses cannot agree on a bill's text. If unresolved before Parliament dissolves, they lapse automatically.

How mediation works: When the Senate and National Assembly pass different versions of a bill, a joint Mediation Committee of equal members from both houses is formed. They have 30 days to agree on a compromise text. If the mediated version is approved by both houses, it goes for Presidential assent. If not, the bill fails for that Parliament.
